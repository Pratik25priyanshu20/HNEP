# v0.3.0 Methodology Notes

Companion document to the v0.3.0 CHANGELOG entry. Documents the two
methodological choices most likely to draw reviewer questions, with the
reasoning we used to make them.

## Why train resampling was not shipped

The v0.3.1 plan originally included full **train + test bootstrap
resampling** in `bootstrap_statistic_ci` — refitting the best surrogate on
a resampled train set per bootstrap iteration, on the theory that the
v0.2 bootstrap (which resampled only the test set) was under-estimating
the headline statistic's finite-sample variance and that this was the
cause of the ~70% / 43% nominal-95% CI coverage we observed during T1.3.

Before implementing the refit, we ran a diagnostic that uncovered two
separate measurement bugs:

1. **The Δ coverage diagnostic averaged the "true value" across seeds**
   that themselves drew β_q freshly per seed. Per-seed CIs were being
   checked against the population *mean* Δ, not the per-seed truth. Each
   seed has its own underlying β_q (and therefore its own Δ_pop), so the
   averaged truth was the wrong target by construction.

2. **The archetype constructors drew `β_q` after `X`**, so the same
   `seed` produced *different* β_q at different `n_samples` — comparing
   CI@n=400 against truth@n=2000 was sampling two different populations.

Both bugs were in the test setup, not in the bootstrap itself. We fixed
them by re-ordering the rng draws so all population parameters precede
the sample realisation (so `seed` alone determines the population and
`n_samples` controls only the realisation depth), and by computing the
truth value per-seed at the same large `n` instead of averaging.

Independently, we also discovered that the v0.2 SurrogationProbe
bootstrap was computing a closed-form proxy
`mean(MSE_per_sample) / mean_d(Var(q_d))` rather than the headline
`1 − mean_d(max(0, R²_d))` — the two differ when `Var(q_d)` varies
across dims. Aligning the bootstrap statistic with the headline removed
this proxy bias for free.

With those three fixes — rng split, per-seed truth, headline-aligned
bootstrap statistic — per-seed CI coverage on the Reg / DW / Ig
archetypes restored to:

| Archetype     | SS coverage | Δ coverage |
|---------------|------------:|-----------:|
| Regularizer   |     100%    |    93%     |
| Dead Weight   |     100%    |    93%     |
| Ignored       |     100%    |   100%     |
| **Combined**  | **100%**    | **96%**    |

Both above the nominal 95% within bootstrap noise. The original coverage
gap was a measurement artifact, not a bootstrap defect. Train resampling
would have solved a problem that didn't exist, at a runtime cost of
~8× per evaluation. Dropped.

## Why thresholds are CI-upper-bound, not point-estimate

The original T1.2 calibration set thresholds at the 99th percentile of
point estimates (SS_obs, Δ_obs) observed on the calibration archetypes
(Regularizer / Dead Weight for SS, Ignored / Dead Weight for Δ). Under
the headline-aligned SS bootstrap in v0.3.0, the SS CI is ~50% wider
than the v0.2 proxy CI. A point-estimate threshold of 0.0859 meant that
~5% of Regularizer / Dead Weight calibration seeds had CI upper bounds
above 0.0859, so their CIs straddled the threshold and the verdict
demoted to Inconclusive — even though the point estimate was clearly
below threshold. The 60-cell verdict accuracy dropped to 83.3%.

The fix shipped in v0.3.0 derives thresholds from the **99th percentile
of CI upper bounds** instead. This is a verdict-stability rule by
construction: for ~99% of calibration seeds, the CI upper bound sits at
or below the threshold, so no straddling. Under this rule:

* SS threshold: 0.099 (was 0.0859 at point estimate)
* Δ threshold: 0.055 (was 0.0235 at point estimate)
* 60-cell verdict accuracy: 100%

The SS threshold sits ~15% higher than a point-estimate calibration —
this is the methodological cost. The benefit is that any model whose SS
CI lies entirely below 0.099 is unambiguously REPLACEABLE, with no
seed-dependent straddling.

This is documented in `Thresholds.from_calibration` so users who
recalibrate against their own corpus understand the rule.
